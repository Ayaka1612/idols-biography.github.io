Early Life and Background

The Weeknd, born Abel Makkonen Tesfaye on February 16, 1990, in Toronto, Canada, is of Ethiopian descent. He was raised by his mother and grandmother after his father left when he was young. Abel dropped out of high school and began pursuing music.


Career Beginnings

In 2010, he started his music career by anonymously uploading songs to YouTube under the name “The Weeknd.” He quickly gained attention for his mysterious persona and unique sound.


Breakthrough Mixtapes

His early success came from a trilogy of critically acclaimed mixtapes: House of Balloons, Thursday, and Echoes of Silence. These were later compiled into the album Trilogy in 2012.


Mainstream Success

The Weeknd’s major breakthrough into the mainstream came with his 2015 album Beauty Behind the Madness, which featured chart-topping hits like “Can’t Feel My Face” and “The Hills.”


Musical Style and Impact

The Weeknd is known for his haunting vocals, dark emotional lyrics, and genre-blending style that mixes R&B, pop, and alternative music. He is considered one of the best-selling music artists of his generation.
