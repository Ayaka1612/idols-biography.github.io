Introduction

Lalisa Manoban, better known by her stage name Lisa, is a Thai rapper, singer, and dancer based in South Korea. She is best known as a member of the globally successful K-pop girl group BLACKPINK.


Personal Background

Born as Pranpriya Manobal, she later legally changed her first name to Lalisa. While her biological parents' names have not been publicly disclosed, her stepfather, Marco Bruschweiler, is a renowned Swiss chef who is active in Thailand.


Early Life and Audition

Lisa showed a strong passion for performance from a young age and was a member of a Thai dance group called We Zaa Cool, which performed locally. In 2010, at just 13 years old, she auditioned for the YG Entertainment Competition in Thailand, where she ranked first.

Training Period

Impressed by her talent, Yang Hyunsuk, the founder of YG Entertainment, offered her a trainee contract, which she accepted. In 2011, Lisa moved to South Korea and underwent five years of intensive training to prepare for her idol career.
